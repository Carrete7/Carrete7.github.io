#2 Heating Oil Energy Content

In the United States, standard #2 Heating Oil has an incredibly high energy density. One US Gallon of heating oil contains approximately 138,500 BTUs of energy. However, because no furnace is 100% efficient, some of this heat escapes through the exhaust flue.

Gallons Consumed: $$ \text{Gallons} = \frac{\text{Load (BTU/hr)} \times \text{Hours}}{\text{138,500} \times \text{Efficiency}} $$

By upgrading from an old 70% efficient boiler to a modern 90% condensing boiler, homeowners and industrial facilities can reduce their gallon consumption and heating bills significantly.
