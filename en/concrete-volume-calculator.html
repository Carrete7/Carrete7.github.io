How to Calculate Cubic Yards for Concrete

In the US construction industry, ready-mix concrete is sold by the Cubic Yard ($yd^3$). To find this volume, you must multiply the Length, Width, and Depth. However, since depth is usually measured in inches, you must convert it to feet before multiplying.

1. Convert Depth to Feet: $$ \text{Depth (ft)} = \frac{\text{Depth (in)}}{12} $$ 2. Calculate Cubic Feet: $$ Volume (ft^3) = \text{Length (ft)} \times \text{Width (ft)} \times \text{Depth (ft)} $$ 3. Convert to Cubic Yards: $$ Volume (yd^3) = \frac{Volume (ft^3)}{27} $$

Pre-mixed Bags Estimation

If you are mixing your own concrete for a small job, you'll buy it in bags. A standard 80 lb bag yields about 0.60 cubic feet of concrete, while a 60 lb bag yields about 0.45 cubic feet.
