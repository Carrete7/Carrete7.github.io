Calculating Metal Weight by Density

Estimating the weight of structural metals is essential for shipping logistics and crane rigging. The calculation is straightforward: find the total volume of the piece in cubic inches, and multiply it by the material's specific density.

General Formula: $$ W (lbs) = \text{Volume (in}^3) \times \text{Density (lbs/in}^3) $$

Common Metal Densities (US Standard)

  • Mild/Carbon Steel: ~0.2833 lbs/in³ (or 490 lbs/ft³)
  • Stainless Steel (300 series): ~0.2890 lbs/in³
  • Aluminum (6061): ~0.0975 lbs/in³
