How to convert BTU/hr to kW?

The British Thermal Unit (BTU) is a measure of heat energy. In the HVAC industry, BTU/hr (BTU per hour) represents the cooling or heating capacity of a system. The Kilowatt (kW) is the standard SI unit used for the same purpose in modern engineering and international specifications.

1 kW = 3,412.14 BTU/hr 1,000 BTU/hr = 0.29307 kW

BTU in HVAC Engineering

Accurate thermal conversion is critical for several technical tasks:

  • Cooling Load Calculation: When sizing an AC unit for a server room or warehouse, you might calculate the load in kW (based on electrical equipment heat) but need to buy a unit rated in BTU.
  • Efficiency Ratings (EER/COP): Converting units allows engineers to compare the efficiency of different manufacturers across international markets.
  • Boiler Capacity: Industrial boilers often list their output in BTU/hr, while energy consumption and utility billing are measured in kW or Therms.

FAQ: Thermal Energy Units

How many BTU is 1 Ton of AC?

In the refrigeration industry, 1 Ton of Refrigeration is equal to exactly 12,000 BTU/hr (or 3.517 kW).

Is BTU the same as kW?

No. BTU is a unit of energy, while kW is a unit of power (energy over time). However, in HVAC, "BTU" is often used as a shorthand for "BTU per hour" to describe cooling capacity.
