How to convert sq ft to sq m?

In international real estate and industrial engineering, converting surface areas is vital for facility management. While the US uses Square Feet (ft²), the majority of the world uses the metric Square Meter (m²) as the standard unit for area.

1 Square Meter = 10.7639 Square Feet 1 Square Foot = 0.092903 Square Meters

Surface Area in Industrial Projects

Why this conversion is a key metric for your operation:

  • Lease & Real Estate: Comparing industrial rental rates ($ per sq ft vs € per sq m) requires precise conversion to assess global facility costs.
  • Lighting & HVAC: Lighting density (Lux) and Cooling loads (BTU/sq ft) are directly calculated based on the floor area. An error here leads to poorly lit or overheated environments.
  • Equipment Footprint: When planning a new production line, imported machinery often has dimensions in metric units. Ensuring they fit within the allocated bay area (measured in ft²) is critical for workflow design.

FAQ: Area Conversion

Is a square meter larger than a square yard?

Yes. A square meter is about 1.196 square yards. Consequently, a square meter is also much larger than a square foot (roughly 10.76 times larger).

How do I convert sq ft to sq m quickly?

A common field shortcut is to divide the square footage by 10.76 to get the area in square meters.
