IndustrialCalc Pro: The Open Engineering Platform

IndustrialCalc Pro is a digital project designed to solve a major problem for technical professionals: the need to perform complex mathematical calculations, unit conversions, and normative queries directly on-site or on the production floor, without relying on heavy desktop manuals or expensive software.

Our platform unifies dozens of interactive tools, based on international standards (IEC, NEC, ASHRAE) and empirical formulation, offering instant and reliable results from any mobile or desktop device.

Technical Areas of Expertise

  • Electrical Engineering & Power: Tools for NEC compliance, including voltage drop calculations, three-phase cross-sectional wire sizing, and reactive power compensation analysis (Power Factor / Capacitor Banks).
  • Fluid Mechanics & Hydraulics: Modules to estimate friction head loss in pipes using the Hazen-Williams equation, as well as sizing the brake horsepower (BHP) required for centrifugal water pumps.
  • Thermodynamics & HVAC: Estimation algorithms for latent and sensible thermal loads to obtain cooling requirements in BTU/h, Tons, and kW, essential for the selection of chillers and VRF systems.
  • Classical Mechanics & Conversions: High-precision converters for motor torque, pressure units, thermal expansion of materials, and concrete volume estimations for industrial foundations.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Are the calculator results accurate?
Yes. Our tools apply standard mathematical and physical formulas used in modern engineering. However, the results are provided as a high-level estimation. For official project signing and stamping, results must always be verified by a licensed professional engineer (PE) according to the real conditions and hidden variables of each specific installation.
Why are there tools for both Metric and Imperial systems?
The global industry operates in a mixed ecosystem. While wire calculation in Europe is governed by mm², in America, the AWG standard is used. Similarly, HVAC equipment manufacturers often express their capacities in BTU/h. Therefore, we offer converters and calculators adapted to both metric realities.
Is there a cost to use the platform?
No, the entire catalog of tools, technical guides, reference tables, and calculators on IndustrialCalc Pro is 100% free and open access, sustained solely through non-intrusive advertising.
