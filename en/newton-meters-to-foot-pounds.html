Torque: Nm to lb-ft

Industrial torque conversion for bolts, motors, and machinery.

How to convert Nm to lb-ft?

Torque is a measure of the rotational force applied to an object. In the International System (SI), the unit is the Newton-meter (Nm). In the United States and the United Kingdom, technical torque is often expressed in Foot-pounds (lb-ft).

Torque in Mechanical Engineering

Why accurate torque conversion is essential for reliability:

  • Clamping Force: Proper bolt tightening ensures that joints remain secure under vibration. Using the wrong scale on a torque wrench can lead to stripped threads or loose parts.
  • Motor Specifications: Gearbox and motor selection depends on the peak torque required to move a load. Most software calculates in Nm, but US motors often list specs in lb-ft.
  • Automotive Tapping: Wheel lug nuts and engine internal components have very specific torque patterns that must be followed according to the manufacturer's scale.

FAQ: Understanding Torque Units

Is lb-ft the same as ft-lb?

Technically, lb-ft (Pound-foot) is the correct unit for torque (static force), while ft-lb (Foot-pound) is often used to describe work or energy. In most industrial workshops, however, they are used interchangeably.

How much is 100 Nm in lb-ft?

100 Newton-meters is equivalent to approximately 73.76 foot-pounds of torque.
