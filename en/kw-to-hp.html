How to convert kW to HP?

In the industrial world, power is measured in two main units: Kilowatts (kW), the standard unit in the International System (SI), and Horsepower (HP), commonly used in the US and for rating mechanical engines and electric motors.

1 kW = 1.341022 HP (Mechanical) 1 HP = 0.745699 kW

Power Units in Engineering

Why this conversion is vital for your facility:

  • Motor Sizing: When replacing an old American motor (rated in HP) with a modern European one (rated in kW), exact matching is required to ensure the mechanical load is handled safely.
  • VFD Configuration: Variable Frequency Drives often require the motor nameplate power to be entered. If the drive is programmed in kW but your motor is HP, accurate conversion avoids tripping.
  • Efficiency Analysis: Calculating the electrical input vs. mechanical output often requires switching between these units to determine the overall system efficiency.

FAQ: Power & Horsepower

Is there a difference between HP and CV?

Yes. HP (Horsepower) is the mechanical/imperial unit (0.7457 kW), while CV (Caballo de Vapor) is the metric horsepower (0.7355 kW). This tool uses the standard Mechanical HP used in the US.

How many kW is a 1 HP motor?

A standard 1 HP electric motor consumes approximately 0.746 kilowatts of power at 100% efficiency.
