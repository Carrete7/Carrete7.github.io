Bulk Material Handling Calculations

In the mining, aggregate, and manufacturing industries, conveyor belt capacity is typically measured in Short Tons Per Hour (TPH). One standard US short ton equals 2,000 lbs.

Conveyor Capacity Formula (TPH): $$ C = \frac{A \times V \times D \times 60}{2000} $$

Variables Explained:

  • $C$ = Capacity in Tons Per Hour (TPH).
  • $A$ = Cross-sectional area of the load in square feet ($ft^2$).
  • $V$ = Belt velocity in Feet Per Minute (FPM).
  • $D$ = Material Bulk Density in pounds per cubic foot ($lbs/ft^3$).
  • 60 = Minutes in an hour.
  • 2000 = Pounds in a Short Ton.
