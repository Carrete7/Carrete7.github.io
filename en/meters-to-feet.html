How to Convert Meters to Feet?

The meter (m) is the base unit of length in the International System of Units (SI), while the foot (ft) is a unit of length in the British Imperial and United States Customary systems. Since 1959, the international foot has been defined as exactly 0.3048 meters.

The Mathematical Formula

To convert meters to feet, you can multiply the value in meters by the conversion factor of 3.2808399:

ft = m × 3.28084

Conversely, to convert from feet back to meters, the formula is:

m = ft × 0.3048

Technical Definition of the Meter

The meter is currently defined as the length of the path travelled by light in a vacuum during a time interval of 1/299,792,458 of a second. This ensures a universal constant for precision engineering and scientific research across all borders.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is a meter longer than a foot?
Yes, a meter is significantly longer. One meter is equal to approximately 3.28 feet. In construction, a quick "rule of thumb" is that a meter is roughly three feet and three inches.

What is the difference between an International Foot and a US Survey Foot?
The International Foot (used in this calculator) is exactly 0.3048 meters. The US Survey Foot is slightly larger (1200/3937 meters), used primarily in historical surveying data in the United States.
