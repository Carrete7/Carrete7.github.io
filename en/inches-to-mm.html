How to convert Inches to Millimeters?

In the world of mechanical engineering and manufacturing, the transition between imperial (inches) and metric (mm) units is a daily task. Since the international yard and pound agreement of 1959, the inch has been defined as exactly 25.4 millimeters.

1 Inch (in) = 25.4 Millimeters (mm) 1 Millimeter (mm) = 0.0393701 Inches (in)

Why Precision Matters in Machining

Understanding the difference between decimal systems is vital for high-tolerance work:

  • CNC Programming: When translating a drawing from imperial to metric, rounding errors can lead to parts that don't fit (interference) or are too loose (slop).
  • Tooling Selection: Using a 13mm wrench on a 1/2" bolt (12.7mm) might work, but it can strip the head over time. Always use the exact tool for the measurement.
  • Global Supply Chain: Aerospace and automotive sectors often use a mix of both systems depending on the component's origin, requiring engineers to be fluent in both.

FAQ: Mechanical Measurements

Is 25mm the same as 1 inch?

No. While 25mm is close, a true inch is 25.4mm. That 0.4mm difference is massive in precision engineering and can cause mechanical failure if ignored.

What is a 'thou' in engineering?

A 'thou' (short for a thousandth of an inch) is 0.001 inches, which equals 0.0254 mm. It is the standard unit of tolerance in many US machine shops.
