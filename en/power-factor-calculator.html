Why Correct Your Power Factor?

Industrial motors, transformers, and HVAC compressors consume Reactive Power (kVAR) to create magnetic fields. This drops the Power Factor (PF) of your facility. Utility companies charge heavy fines if your PF drops below 0.90 because they have to supply more Apparent Power (kVA) to your building.

By installing a capacitor bank, you supply the reactive power locally, bringing the PF close to 1.0 and eliminating the fines.

Correction Formula: $$ kVAR = kW \times (\tan(\arccos(PF_{current})) - \tan(\arccos(PF_{target}))) $$
