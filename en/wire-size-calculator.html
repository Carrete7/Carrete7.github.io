How is Wire Size and Voltage Drop Calculated?

Sizing electrical conductors correctly is critical for safety and equipment efficiency. According to the National Electrical Code (NEC), a maximum voltage drop of 3% is recommended for branch circuits, and a total of 5% for the feeder and branch circuit combined.

Our calculator uses the exact specific resistivity constant ($K$) for conductors at 75°C (167°F) to ensure real-world accuracy. The constant $K$ is 12.9 ohms-cmil/ft for Copper and 21.2 ohms-cmil/ft for Aluminum.

The Formulas Used

To determine the minimum Circular Mils (cmil) required, we use the following standard engineering formulas depending on your electrical system:

For Direct Current (DC) and Single-Phase AC: $$ CMIL = \frac{2 \times K \times I \times L}{V_{d}} $$
For Three-Phase AC: $$ CMIL = \frac{\sqrt{3} \times K \times I \times L}{V_{d}} $$

Where:

  • $K$ = Specific resistivity of the conductor material.
  • $I$ = Load current in Amperes.
  • $L$ = One-way length of the circuit in feet.
  • $V_{d}$ = Allowable voltage drop in Volts (e.g., 3% of 120V = 3.6V).
