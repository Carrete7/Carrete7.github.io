NEC Voltage Drop Formulas

The National Electrical Code (NEC) recommends a maximum voltage drop of 3% for branch circuits and 5% for feeders to ensure equipment operates efficiently and safely.

To find the proper wire size in Circular Mils (CM), we use the standard K-Factor method:

1-Phase Calculation: $$ CM = \frac{2 \times K \times I \times D}{V_{drop}} $$ 3-Phase Calculation: $$ CM = \frac{1.732 \times K \times I \times D}{V_{drop}} $$

Variables:

  • $CM$ = Wire area in Circular Mils.
  • $K$ = Direct Current Resistance constant (12.9 for Copper, 21.2 for Aluminum).
  • $I$ = Current in Amperes.
  • $D$ = One-way distance in Feet.
  • $V_{drop}$ = Maximum allowable voltage drop in Volts (e.g., $120V \times 0.03 = 3.6V$).
