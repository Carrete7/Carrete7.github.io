How to Calculate Battery Backup Time

To accurately determine how long a battery will last, you must calculate its total energy in Watt-hours (Wh), apply the safe Depth of Discharge (DoD) for your specific battery chemistry, and account for the power loss inside the DC-to-AC inverter.

Usable Energy (Wh): $$ E_{usable} = Ah \times V \times DoD $$ Runtime (Hours): $$ \text{Hours} = \frac{E_{usable} \times \eta_{inverter}}{\text{Load (Watts)}} $$

Important Note on DoD: Discharging a Lead-Acid or AGM battery below 50% of its capacity will permanently damage it and shorten its lifespan. Lithium-ion (LiFePO4) batteries can safely be discharged up to 80-90%.
