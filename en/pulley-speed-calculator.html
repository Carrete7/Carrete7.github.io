Belt Drive Mathematics

In a standard belt and pulley system, the rotational speed (RPM) is inversely proportional to the pulley diameter. If the driven pulley is twice as large as the drive pulley, it will rotate at half the speed.

Pulley Ratio Equation: $$ D_1 \times RPM_1 = D_2 \times RPM_2 $$

Belt Speed (FPM): Engineers also need to ensure the V-belt isn't traveling too fast for its rating. Belt velocity is calculated in Feet Per Minute (FPM) using the diameter of the drive pulley:

$$ V = \frac{D_1 \times \pi}{12} \times RPM_1 $$
