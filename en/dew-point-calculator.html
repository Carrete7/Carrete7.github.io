The Magnus-Tetens Formula

The dew point is the temperature to which air must be cooled to become saturated with water vapor. When cooled further, the airborne water vapor will condense to form liquid water (dew). This is a critical metric for HVAC engineers, compressed air dryers, and painting/coating facilities.

Our calculator uses the highly accurate Magnus-Tetens formula to approximate the dew point ($T_d$):

$$ \alpha = \ln\left(\frac{RH}{100}\right) + \frac{17.625 \times T}{243.04 + T} $$
$$ T_d = \frac{243.04 \times \alpha}{17.625 - \alpha} $$

Note: The formula relies on Celsius values internally. The calculator automatically converts your Fahrenheit inputs to Celsius for the equation and converts the result back to Fahrenheit.
