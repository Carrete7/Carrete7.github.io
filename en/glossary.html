Electrical Engineering

AWG (American Wire Gauge)
A standardized wire gauge system used predominantly in North America for the diameters of round, solid, nonferrous, electrically conducting wire.
Calculate AWG Size
Apparent Power (kVA)
The combination of real power (kW) and reactive power (kVAR). It represents the total power that the utility must provide to the system.
Check Apparent Power
Full Load Amps (FLA)
The amount of current a motor or transformer draws when operating at its rated horsepower or kVA capacity.
Calculate FLA
Power Factor (PF)
The ratio of real power flowing to the load to the apparent power in the circuit. It is a measure of electrical efficiency.
Calculate Efficiency
Voltage Drop
The decrease in electrical potential along the path of a current flowing in an electrical circuit, typically caused by wire resistance over long distances.
Run Voltage Drop Analysis

Mechanical & Fluids

Brake Horsepower (BHP)
The actual amount of real horsepower required by a pump or machine to perform its task, accounting for mechanical efficiency losses.
Calculate BHP
Total Dynamic Head (TDH)
The total equivalent height that a fluid is to be pumped, taking into account friction losses in the pipe and vertical lift.
Estimate Friction Loss
Specific Gravity
The ratio of the density of a substance to the density of a standard, usually water for liquids.
Adjust for Density
Torque
A measure of the force that can cause an object to rotate about an axis. In motors, it is the rotational equivalent of linear force.
Calculate Torque

HVAC & Thermal

BTU (British Thermal Unit)
The amount of heat required to raise the temperature of one pound of water by one degree Fahrenheit. Used to measure AC capacity.
BTU Load Calculator
ACH (Air Changes per Hour)
A measure of the air volume added to or removed from a space in one hour, divided by the volume of the space.
Calculate ACH
Dew Point
The atmospheric temperature (varying according to pressure and humidity) below which water droplets begin to condense.
Dew Point Analysis
U-Value
The rate of transfer of heat through a structure (which can be a single material or a composite), divided by the difference in temperature across that structure.
Calculate Insulation

Solar & Renewables

Peak Sun Hours (PSH)
The equivalent number of hours per day when solar irradiance averages 1,000 W/m². Crucial for sizing solar arrays.
Solar Sizing Tool
Derate Factor
A multiplier used to account for real-world losses in solar energy systems (soiling, shading, wiring losses).
Read Sizing Guide
Payback Period
The amount of time it takes for an investment in a renewable energy system to generate enough savings to cover its initial cost.
