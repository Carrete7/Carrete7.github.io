The Hazen-Williams Equation

For water systems at standard temperatures, engineers rely on the empirical Hazen-Williams formula to calculate friction loss. It is the industry standard in the US for designing sprinkler systems, HVAC chilled water loops, and plumbing networks.

Friction Loss Equation (PSI): $$ P_{d} = \frac{4.52 \times Q^{1.852}}{C^{1.852} \times d^{4.87}} \times L $$

Where $P_d$ = Pressure drop in PSI, $Q$ = Flow in GPM, $C$ = Roughness coefficient, $d$ = Internal diameter in inches, and $L$ = Length in feet.
